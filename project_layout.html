Project Process Recipe Setting for Success

Working efficiently on meaningful projects is my guiding principle. I find true fulfillment when my efforts generate real value for the organizations, clients, and society I serve. To consistently deliver high-quality results on time, I have developed my own methodology for enhancing project efficiency.

✅ 1. Accept work, ask for expected standards upfront

When a leader assigns work, I make sure to clarify the standards before proceeding. I will ask: "What effect does the leader hope to achieve with this project?"

✅ 2. Use the SMART formula to structure the project

  • Specific: Clearly define specific indicators and details.
  • Measurable: Ensure the goals can be quantified.
  • Achievable: The goal should be realistic and attainable.
  • Relevant: It should be related to the overall objective.
  • Time-bound: Specify a clear completion time.

✅ 3. Unsure about correct direction, bring my thoughts

When encountering problems at work, don't just ask the leader what to do; instead, come up with at least two solutions.

✅ 4. Use the RIDE model to analyze possible solutions

  • R - Risk: Explain the risks and current difficulties.
  • I - Interest: Highlight benefits, and bring in shared interests.
  • D - Difference: Compare and contrast Plan A and Plan B.
  • E - Effect: Mention impacts and point out some shortcomings (it’s better to point out some flaws than to be overly optimistic).

✅ 5. Report work, focus on results

  • Point: Start with the conclusion, emphasize the key points.
  • Reason: Explain the reasons, and provide convincing evidence.
  • Example: Provide an example, it’s best to give a real case.
  • Point (Reiterate): Summarize scattered thoughts and reinforce the viewpoint.

Share work, explain the process. When the leader asks me to share my experience with the team, I make sure to conduct a multi-dimensional analysis instead of speaking vaguely.

✅ 6. Use Business universal analysis to depict a full picture

  • What: Product background
  • Who: Target audience
  • Why: Expected goals
  • Where: Usage scenarios
  • When: Demand timeline
  • How: How to verify
  • How much: How many resources to use

✅ 7. GRAI - Project review to improve SOP and share experience

  • G - Goal: Review the goals.
  • R - Result: Evaluate the results, validate the direction, verify with data.
  • A - Analysis: Analyze the reasons for success/failure, both surface/deep reasons, contingencies, and inevitabilities.
  • I - Insight: Summarize the methodology.

Never repeat at a low level. I apply SOP and validate my methodologies to improve my ability to think more critically.
