Getting to Know Cindy(Siyu)

Introduction

I grew up in Tianjin, a northeastern coastal city in China, just 30 minutes from Beijing by high-speed rail. Until high school, I lived a fairly traditional life, studying, resting, and enjoying my hobby of building 3D papercraft puzzle models. However, my family's love for travel exposed me to 13 countries and countless Chinese cities by the time I was 15. This unique and adventurous upbringing sparked my motivation to apply for the AFS Intercultural Program and gave me the courage to study in the U.S. alone at a young age.

Fortunately, I was welcomed by a loving host family in Naselle, Washington, where I experienced life as a “Jewish kid” and a Washingtonian. My 10-month stay introduced me to the thought-provoking and diverse American education system. I embraced every opportunity to try something new—volleyball, softball, knowledge bowl, woodworking, and musical drama. These experiences ignited my desire to continue my education in the U.S. and to keep exploring.

And so, I did! After visiting a private Catholic high school, Bishop Blanchet, in the Green Lake area of Seattle, I knew it was the right place for me. My intuition proved correct. With the support of the school's inclusive culture, I thrived on campus—serving as the president of the Business Opportunity Club, a member of the Women’s Lacrosse team, and an international student ambassador.

As I approached my college decision, my adventurous spirit led me to explore the city of Boston in the East Coast and Northeastern University, a school known for its experiential and interdisciplinary learning. I knew I wanted to study something that combined science and business, and the combination of Data Science—a rapidly growing technology field in the 21st century —and Supply Chain Management—the critical business bottleneck highlighted by the pandemic—felt like the perfect fit for me to acquire the skillset for challenging problem-solving.

Now, in the final year of my undergraduate studies, I feel nothing but satisfaction with my major and career choices. Just as building 3D papercraft puzzle models requires understanding how each small piece fits into the overall design, supply chain operations demand insight into how each process contributes to the entire system's dynamics.
