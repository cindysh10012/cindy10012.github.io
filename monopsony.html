Assessing the Impact of Monopsony Power in the Publishing Industry

The Northeastern University Data Initiative and University of Toronto Rotman Business School Collaborative Research aims to investigate the role of monopsony power in the publishing industry, particularly examining how mergers between major publishers, such as Penguin Random House (PRH) and Simon & Schuster (SS), impact various market agents. The study will address concerns about market power gained over book authors and its implications on the industry. Understanding these dynamics is crucial not only for regulatory bodies and policymakers but also for authors, publishers, and consumers. The findings will contribute to the broader discourse on the economic impacts of mergers and provide insights into maintaining a competitive and fair market environment.
